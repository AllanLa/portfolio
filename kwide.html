Keen Winter Intedisciplinary Design

KWIDE stands for Keen Winter Intedisciplinary Design which is a 1.5 week intensive program at Bucknell offered in January. Students give up part of their winter break to prototype and pitch a device that can be a solution or help a current social problem. During my second year at Bucknell, I participated in this program. The program taught us creative brainstorming techniques and growth mindset to help face tough social issues with no existing solutions. My team selected farmlands as our site of interest. Farmhands in developing countries are expected to complete hours of rigorous work every day. After time, this can take aserious toll on the body. This is especially concerning because many of the workers are children and will have to endure these working conditions for their entire lives. In fact, young workers make up about a quarter of the agricultural workforce in developing countries. Pair this with the limited access to quality health care in these rural areas, and the strenuous labor farmers face becomes a very serious decent work issue.

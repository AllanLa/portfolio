Meta

I joined Meta on December 6th, 2021 as an E4 Software Engineer. It's been about three full years into my career at this point. Funny enough, while I was interviewing, the company was known as Facebook at the time. At Meta, I was a part of the Reality Labs organization where I worked on Project Aria.

Leverging my experience at Microsoft, onboarding and ramping up was not as scary as it use to be compared to when I was first entering the industry out of college. Like Microsoft, my role on the Aria Companion App Team was that of a fullstack engineer. I was essentially hired to build a notification system from scratch. We needed notifications for various use cases such as when the glasses finish uploading a video to our servers, and our servers need to talk back to the mobile app paired with the glasses. In my year on the team, I managed to deliver the feature and saw impact immediately through metrics captured by my logging work! On the frontend we used React Native. I would work on our iOS & Android app building out features, native work, and UI. On the backend, I would be creating new or extending key buisness logic for features that I was responsible for. GraphQL was the main backend tech stack that I used and leverged including the frontend.

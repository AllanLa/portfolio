Terrorist Suicide Attacks Visualizations

What is the most effective way to visualize the vast amount of data present in the world? In this project, my team and I were tasked with using two different lenses — clear and persuasive communication — to visualize the same dataset. For our clearly communicated visual, we set out to create a simple graph that gives the audience unbiased and in-depth information on the dataset. On the other hand, our persuasive visual aims to represent the data in the most compelling way possible. We chose to use a data set on suicide attacks. Our goal was to show the user the location and amount of people killed in the suicide attacks, with a focus on Iraq because it is the most impacted. Our persuasive visualization took a map and used a javascript library called P5 to show overtime on the map where suicide attacks occur. Each time an attack occurs at a location, the area highlighted in red. The red vanishes but yellow may take it's place if it is attacked enough. Sound was also used to increase the persuasive factor.
